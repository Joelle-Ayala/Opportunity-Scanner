# Marketing / Advertising / Content / Web Services Industry Evidence Brief

**Canonical industry slug:** `marketing-advertising-content-web-services`

**Customer-facing name:** Marketing / Advertising / Content / Web Services

**Research date:** July 13, 2026

**Agent:** Product Strategy & Product Marketing research agent

**Purpose:** Define an implementation-ready vertical for classifying companies, finding official public-sector evidence, and translating that evidence into qualified Opportunity Action Table rows. This is product research, not a market-size estimate or a claim that any company is eligible for a particular procurement.

## Product recommendation

Use this vertical for service firms whose primary offer is strategy, creative, communications, audience engagement, content, media, or website work. The public-sector demand is real and broader than the commercial phrase "marketing agency": GSA's current Multiple Award Schedule includes video/film production, graphic design, web-based marketing, marketing consulting, advertising, public relations, and marketing research. Its web-based marketing scope expressly includes website design and maintenance, interactive and email marketing, online media management, webcasting, and Section 508 work.

Do not reduce this vertical to advertising bids. The stronger product experience should find four different evidence states:

1. active agency or local-government procurements;
2. pre-solicitation and forecast signals;
3. historical awards, incumbents, vehicles, and prime-partner routes; and
4. funded-recipient or program signals that require downstream procurement validation.

The brief should seed a future industry playbook, sample report, and SEO cluster only after product owners approve implementation. No implementation is included here.

| Evidence state | Default motion | Override condition |
|---|---|---|

## Buyer, partner, and contact-path logic

| Buyer / partner type | Typical roles | Correct first contact path | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Federal contracting office | Contracting officer, contract specialist, small-business specialist | Use the source-native notice contact and the method permitted by the notice; ask scope or process questions before the question deadline | Cold outreach to unrelated program staff after a solicitation restricts communications |
| Requiring program office | Communications director, public affairs officer, recruitment lead, program manager, COR, digital-service lead | For pre-solicitation research, use published industry-day/RFI routes; after release, route acquisition questions through the contracting officer | Treating the program office as authority to change a solicitation |
| State/local procurement office | Buyer, procurement analyst, supplier diversity officer, department contract manager | Register in the official portal, subscribe to commodity categories, attend pre-proposal events, and use the listed buyer contact | Assuming SAM registration covers local portals |
| Digital/website owner | CIO/CTO office, web manager, digital services, content lead, Section 508/accessibility coordinator | Validate platform, hosting, accessibility, security, content migration, analytics, and maintenance ownership before enrichment | Pitching a visual redesign without technical and accessibility delivery evidence |
| Awarded prime or vehicle holder | Program director, partner/alliance lead, subcontracts manager, small-business liaison | Research the award and vehicle, then approach the prime with a named work package and relevant past performance | Generic "can we subcontract?" outreach based only on a large ceiling |
| Funded recipient | Executive/program director, communications lead, procurement/grants manager | Verify the funded scope and recipient procurement policy; lead with the specific funded objective and procurement question | Treating the grant total as spendable agency revenue |
| Community or language partner | Community engagement lead, coalition manager, language-access lead | Partner when local trust, language, or facilitation is material to delivery; define roles and data/brand approvals | Using community organizations as a superficial proposal credential |
| Media/platform/production partner | Media owner, authorized representative, production partner, local crew, accessibility vendor | Use when exclusive inventory, local production, specialized equipment, or accessible formats are required | Misclassifying a sole-source media-owner notice as an open agency competition |

Contact enrichment should follow target classification. Preserve a notice's source-native contact. Use enrichment for a company-domain target such as a prime or funded recipient, not as a substitute for an official procurement route.

## Likely NAICS codes, with caveats

These are search candidates, not automatic company classifications. Under FAR 19.102, the contracting officer assigns the NAICS code that best describes the principal purpose of the procurement, normally considering the greatest share of contract value. A company may legitimately match several codes, but each solicitation can use a different principal-purpose code.

| NAICS | Official industry / relevant use | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| 541810 | Advertising Agencies | Integrated campaign creation and placement are the principal service |
| 541820 | Public Relations Agencies | PR strategy and implementation, media relations, or public-information support dominate |
| 541613 | Marketing Consulting Services | Marketing strategy, objectives, policy, planning, or campaign advisory work dominates |
| 541910 | Marketing Research and Public Opinion Polling | Systematic audience, market, opinion, survey, focus-group, or message-testing research dominates |
| 541430 | Graphic Design Services | Visual communication, publication, identity, illustration, or graphic production dominates |
| 541511 | Custom Computer Programming Services | Custom website development, testing, and support dominate; GSA uses the related SIN for web-based marketing, but the notice controls |
| 512110 | Motion Picture and Video Production | Producing or producing/distributing video, film, television, or commercials dominates |
| 541830 | Media Buying Agencies | Purchasing and reselling advertising time or space is the principal activity |
| 541850 | Indoor and Outdoor Display Advertising | Creating or placing public display advertising is the principal activity |
| 541890 | Other Services Related to Advertising | A specialized advertising service does not fit the more specific 5418 industries |
| 561920 | Convention and Trade Show Organizers | Conference, meeting, event, or trade-show organization is the principal service |
| 541930 | Translation and Interpretation Services | Language translation or interpretation, rather than campaign strategy, is the principal service |

Do not treat a NAICS match as proof of set-aside eligibility, schedule status, technical fit, or an active opportunity. Check the solicitation's current size standard, set-aside, amendments, and scope.

## Qualification and disqualifier logic

### Hard gates

Reject or downgrade before scoring when any of these is true:

- The record is cancelled, expired, archived, or superseded and is being presented as a live opportunity.
- A sources-sought notice or RFI is being presented as an offer or guaranteed procurement.
- A sole-source or brand/exclusive-inventory notice has no credible challenge, reseller, media-owner, or subcontract path.
- The company cannot meet the set-aside, registration, vehicle, place-of-performance, clearance, insurance, or submission requirements.
- The required work is primarily outside the company's capabilities, such as software product delivery, printing/fabrication, event talent, or political/lobbying work.
- The company cannot meet mandatory Section 508, accessible-document/video, language-access, privacy, data, security, CMS, or hosting requirements.
- The pursuit requires local crews, community credibility, specialized equipment, or multilingual capacity that the company cannot provide or partner for.
- A grant or assistance award lacks a validated downstream buying need, eligible procurement path, or current project period.
- The opportunity economics rely on treating a contract ceiling, potential value, total award, or grant amount as available revenue.
- Political or lobbying activity is bundled into a federal cost-reimbursement scope without a separable, allowable service and specialist review. FAR 31.205-22 makes specified lobbying and political activity costs unallowable, subject to stated exceptions.

### Actionability score

Score only after hard gates. Preserve evidence for every score.

| Dimension | Weight | Full-credit evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| Exact service and mission fit | 25 | Required deliverables match named capabilities and relevant outcomes |
| Status and timing | 20 | Current record, workable deadline, amendments checked, next event known |
| Route access | 20 | Registration/set-aside/vehicle/portal/partner path is available and verified |
| Past performance and capacity | 15 | Comparable scope, audience, scale, staffing, geography, and delivery model |
| Compliance and production readiness | 15 | Accessibility, privacy/security, rights, language, review, hosting, media, and on-site gates are understood and feasible |
| Evidence confidence | 5 | Official source, dates, status, contact, and controlling attachments are present |

- **High Actionability:** 75-100, no hard disqualifier, and one owned next action can occur within 10 business days.
- **Medium Actionability:** 50-74, or one material but solvable access/capability gap remains.
- **Low Actionability:** below 50, historical/policy evidence without a current route, or several unresolved gates.

### Required next-action mapping

- Active solicitation: complete bid/no-bid review and submit questions or proposal through the official route.
- RFI/sources sought: decide whether to send a compliant capability response; create a recheck for the forecast or solicitation.
- Historical award: identify incumbent, ordering office, period end, competition history, and vehicle before outreach.
- Prime route: define a named work package and contact the small-business liaison, subcontracts lead, or program partner role.
- Funded buyer: validate approved scope and procurement route before enriching contacts.
- Website work: run a Section 508/accessibility and technical-delivery readiness check before marking high actionability.
- Local procurement: complete portal and commodity registration before the response deadline.
- Policy/program signal: name the conversion event and next-check date; do not create an outreach task without a target and reason.

## Three SEO article briefs

### 1. How to Find Government Marketing and Advertising RFPs Without Searching Only for "Marketing"

**Primary keyword:** government marketing RFPs

**Secondary keywords:** government advertising contracts; public outreach RFP; communications contracts; SAM.gov marketing opportunities

**Intent:** Consideration / high-intent educational

**Thesis:** Public-sector demand is distributed across advertising, public relations, recruitment, public information, outreach, research, content, and mission-specific terminology. A useful search combines translated scope terms with NAICS/PSC candidates, agency, status, and official attachments.

**Outline:**

1. Why titles and one NAICS code miss relevant work.
2. Translate commercial services into agency language.
3. Separate active solicitations, RFIs, sole-source notices, and award history.
4. Use SAM.gov for current notices and USAspending for buyer/incumbent research.
5. Turn one source record into target, motion, contact path, caveat, and next action.

**Official proof set:** SAM.gov Contract Opportunities; FAR 10 and 15.201; Census NAICS; Acquisition.gov PSC manual; USAspending Federal Spending Guide.

**CTA:** Scan an agency or creative-services website to produce a sourced Opportunity Action Table.

### 2. Can a Web Design Agency Sell Website Redesign and UX Services to Government?

**Primary keyword:** government website design contracts

**Secondary keywords:** government website redesign RFP; Section 508 web development; public sector UX contracts

**Intent:** Consideration / qualification

**Thesis:** Yes, but design fit is only the opening gate. Public-sector website work can include custom development, CMS/content migration, accessibility, user research, analytics, security/hosting boundaries, and ongoing maintenance. The controlling solicitation, not a portfolio label or NAICS guess, defines the pursuit.

**Outline:**

1. How GSA describes web-based marketing and website maintenance.
2. Why website work can appear under 541511, DA01, communications, or another principal-purpose classification.
3. Section 508 and accessibility evidence agencies may request.
4. Prime, vehicle-holder, and local-portal routes for agencies without federal past performance.
5. A readiness checklist: CMS, accessibility testing, content, hosting/security, maintenance, and comparable past performance.

**Official proof set:** GSA MAS marketing and PR category; FAR 19.102; Section508.gov procurement guidance; U.S. Web Design System; Acquisition.gov PSC manual.

**CTA:** Run a fit scan before investing in registration or a proposal.

### 3. Public Outreach Contracts: When to Sell to an Agency, a Prime, or a Funded Buyer

**Primary keyword:** public outreach contracts

**Secondary keywords:** community engagement RFP; government communications contracts; funded buyer outreach services

**Intent:** Consideration / product differentiation

**Thesis:** The same outreach capability can travel through three different commercial routes. The source record must identify who controls the work and money: an agency procurement, a prime's subcontract, or a recipient's downstream implementation.

**Outline:**

1. What public outreach can mean: public information, stakeholder engagement, recruitment, benefits enrollment, research, language access, and media.
2. Direct agency route and source-native procurement contact.
3. Prime/vehicle-holder route and separable work packages.
4. Funded-recipient route and why award amount is not vendor budget.
5. Qualification gates: local presence, language, accessibility, neutrality, data/privacy, and procurement status.

**Official proof set:** USAspending recipient and award guidance; SBA prime/subcontracting guidance; GSA subcontracting resources; Cal eProcure; Philadelphia Contracts Hub; FAR 31.205-22 caveat for lobbying/political activity.

**CTA:** Generate the correct revenue motion and contact path for each outreach signal.

## Implementation recommendations

1. Add this vertical only after a regression set includes at least one integrated agency, one web/UX agency, one PR/outreach firm, and one video studio. Their reports should produce visibly different lane mixes.
2. Seed search with translated scope language plus NAICS/PSC candidates; never require a code match when title, description, and attachments provide stronger scope evidence.
3. Treat `notice_type`, `source_status`, `deadline`, `set_aside`, `place_of_performance`, `naics`, `psc`, `vehicle`, and `source_native_contact` as evidence fields, not generated copy.
4. Add vertical-specific qualification fields for `accessibility_readiness`, `website_delivery_boundary`, `media_buying_role`, `production_geography`, `language_access`, `community_delivery`, `rights_clearance`, and `political_lobbying_exclusion`.
5. Keep the free report credible with two or three real records across different evidence types. Lock the deeper buyer/partner map, incumbent route, compliance gates, CRM note, outreach angle, and workflow payload.
6. Do not claim a market size from filtered USAspending totals without a documented query, time period, award types, action/obligation treatment, de-duplication method, and known data-delay caveats.
