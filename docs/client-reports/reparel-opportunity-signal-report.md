# Reparel Opportunity Signal Report

Report date: July 5, 2026  
Prepared for: Reparel leadership  
Source basis: Opportunity Scanner scan data and the public-source records linked in this report.

## Executive Summary

Opportunity Scanner found a clear public-sector money-flow pattern around rehabilitation supplies, physical therapy supplies, orthotic supplies, and VA/healthcare purchasing channels. The strongest near-term path for Reparel is not a broad grant application strategy. It is a targeted channel and buyer strategy: identify distributors and procurement offices already receiving public dollars for rehab, physical therapy, orthotic, and DME-adjacent supply categories, then position Reparel as a reimbursable recovery sleeve product that can fit into existing supply channels.

The highest-priority signals are HHS-funded rehabilitation supply BPAs that run into 2026-2027, followed by VA and defense orthotics purchasing evidence. These are not open solicitations by themselves, but they show where public-sector purchasing is already happening and which distributor or procurement paths should be researched first.

Recommended first move: build a short target list from the top three current/recent supply awards, validate whether each recipient is a distributor, reseller, or direct supplier, and begin outreach to category managers, rehab supply buyers, and agency procurement contacts within 7 days.

## CEO Handoff: What To Do First

Use this report as a channel and buyer-mapping sprint.

1. Start with First Nations Distribution, Performance Health Supply, and National Environmental because they are tied to current or recent HHS rehab/medical supply buying.
2. Validate whether each target is a distributor, reseller, catalog supplier, or direct service provider before sending a broad pitch.
3. Lead with product-category fit: recovery sleeve, post-op support, brace/boot undersleeve, pain/swelling support, PT/rehab supply, and reimbursable DME-adjacent positioning where applicable.
4. Use VA and DOD records as market evidence for prosthetics, orthotics, and physical therapy supply routes, then look for the current buying office or active vehicle.

## Signal Detail

### 1. HHS Medical and Rehabilitation Supplies BPA: First Nations Distribution

- Why it matters: HHS awarded First Nations Distribution $46,400 for a BPA covering miscellaneous medical and rehabilitation supplies and materials. The award period runs from March 28, 2025 to March 31, 2027, which makes this a current channel-research target as of this report date.
- Revenue motion: Sell to Award Recipient; Channel / Distributor Motion
- Target organization: First Nations Distribution LLC, with Department of Health and Human Services as the public buyer.
- Source: USAspending.gov
- Source URL: https://www.usaspending.gov/award/75H71025P00458
- Actionability: High Actionability
- Next best action: Confirm whether First Nations Distribution carries rehab, DME, orthotic, or clinical supplies that overlap with Reparel's sleeves. If yes, approach the company as a channel partner and ask how products are added to public-sector BPA ordering catalogs.
- Contact path: Start with the award recipient's sales, government contracts, or medical supply category team. In parallel, identify the HHS/IHS buying office connected to the award.
- Recommended roles: Government Contracts Manager, Medical Supply Category Manager, Clinical Supply Manager, Procurement Specialist.
- Outreach angle: "Reparel offers clinically positioned recovery sleeves for pain, swelling, post-op recovery, and brace/boot undersleeve use. We are evaluating whether the product fits existing rehab supply BPA ordering paths and would like to understand your supplier onboarding process."

### 2. HHS Medical and Rehabilitation Supplies BPA: Performance Health Supply

- Why it matters: HHS awarded Performance Health Supply $21,600 for miscellaneous medical and rehabilitation supplies and materials. The award period runs from April 9, 2025 to April 13, 2027. This is a strong fit because Performance Health appears directly tied to rehabilitation supply distribution.
- Revenue motion: Sell to Award Recipient; Channel / Distributor Motion
- Target organization: Performance Health Supply LLC, with Department of Health and Human Services as the public buyer.
- Source: USAspending.gov
- Source URL: https://www.usaspending.gov/award/75H71025P00547
- Actionability: High Actionability
- Next best action: Research Performance Health's public-sector catalog, rehab product categories, and supplier onboarding route. Prioritize this target if Reparel wants distribution into existing clinical supply channels rather than direct agency sales.
- Contact path: Recipient first, then public buying office if distributor validation is positive.
- Recommended roles: Rehab Supply Category Manager, DME/Orthotics Product Manager, Government Sales Lead, Clinical Supply Buyer.
- Outreach angle: "Your public-sector rehab supply work appears adjacent to Reparel's recovery sleeve line. We would like to explore whether Reparel can support post-op, arthritis, brace undersleeve, and physical therapy recovery use cases through your public-sector supply channels."

### 3. HHS Physical Rehabilitation BPA: National Environmental

- Why it matters: HHS awarded National Environmental $20,000 for a 2025 physical rehabilitation BPA to purchase required physical therapy supplies for direct patient care on an as-needed basis. The award period runs from September 19, 2025 to September 18, 2026, so this is time-sensitive.
- Revenue motion: Sell to Award Recipient; Sell to Agency
- Target organization: National Environmental Inc., with Department of Health and Human Services as the public buyer.
- Source: USAspending.gov
- Source URL: https://www.usaspending.gov/award/75H71025P01050
- Actionability: High Actionability
- Next best action: Verify whether the award supports a clinic, facility, or IHS/HHS ordering environment, then determine whether Reparel's recovery sleeves can be positioned as physical therapy or recovery support supplies.
- Contact path: Identify National Environmental's government contract point of contact and the HHS buying office. If the recipient is not a relevant medical distributor, pivot to the agency procurement office and use the record as evidence of current demand.
- Recommended roles: Government Contracts Manager, Physical Therapy Supply Buyer, Rehab Services Director, Clinical Supply Manager.
- Outreach angle: "Reparel may fit the as-needed physical rehabilitation supply category, particularly where patients need non-compressive sleeves for pain, swelling, post-op recovery, and use under braces or boots."

- Recommended roles: VA Prosthetics Representative, VA Physical Therapy Supply Buyer, Procurement Specialist, Clinical Supply Manager.
- Outreach angle: "Reparel is already positioned around recovery, inflammation, post-op use, and reimbursable clinical support. We are researching VA physical therapy supply routes where recovery sleeves or brace undersleeves may fit patient care needs."

## Lower-Priority Market Evidence

Three large DMEPOS infrastructure records appeared in the scan: Softrams, Empower AI, and CGS Administrators. These records are useful for understanding Medicare/DME reimbursement and administrative infrastructure, but they are not near-term Reparel sales leads. They should not be pursued as buyer targets unless a separate reimbursement or policy strategy is opened.

Adaptive Driving Alliance also appeared as a VA veteran rehabilitation award. It is relevant to the broader veteran rehabilitation market, but the award centered on a modified vehicle purchase, so it should remain lower priority than rehab supply and orthotic supply records.

## Recommended 7-Day Action Plan

### Day 1: Confirm Product-Lane Positioning

Finalize a one-page public-sector positioning note for Reparel:

- Recovery sleeves for pain, swelling, injury recovery, and post-op support.
- Brace and boot undersleeve applications.
- PDAC-approved reimbursable positioning where applicable.
- Fit with physical therapy, rehabilitation, orthotic, DME, VA prosthetics, and clinical supply channels.

### Day 2: Build the Target Account Sheet

Create a target sheet with First Nations Distribution, Performance Health Supply, National Environmental, Apex Integrated Distribution, and Footmaxx of Virginia. For each, add website/domain, public-sector contract evidence, likely role, buyer hypothesis, and first contact route.

### Day 3: Validate Channel Fit

Research whether the top three recipients are distributors, resellers, catalog suppliers, or direct service providers. Keep only targets with a credible product onboarding route in the first outreach wave.

### Day 4: Identify Buying Offices

For each HHS and VA/DOD record, identify the buying office or procurement path attached to the award. If the buying office cannot be found quickly, create a manual research task rather than delaying outreach to validated distributors.

### Day 5: Prepare Outreach

Draft two outreach versions:

- Distributor version: supplier onboarding, product/category fit, rehab supply catalog fit.
- Agency/procurement version: source capability, recovery sleeve use cases, where to find ordering or vendor-registration guidance.

### Day 6: Send First Outreach and Log Responses

Contact the highest-confidence distributor/channel targets first. Log contact name, role, response, next step, and whether a product sheet, reimbursement note, or clinical evidence packet was requested.

### Day 7: Decide the Pursuit Path

Choose one of three next moves:

- Channel path: pursue supplier onboarding with one or more medical/rehab distributors.
- Agency path: map VA/HHS/DOD procurement offices and look for active sources-sought or solicitation records.
- Evidence path: build a reimbursement/clinical procurement packet before broader outreach.

## Source Coverage and Data Limitations

This report uses Opportunity Scanner scan data completed June 26, 2026, with company website data, USAspending.gov records, and Federal Register-style source categories.

The top records are mostly USAspending.gov award records. They show public-sector money flow and buyer/channel evidence, but they are not open solicitations unless separately verified. Several records have end dates in the past and are labeled as market evidence rather than immediate leads.

Additional rehab and orthotics distributors should be checked during the next source refresh before being added to a pursuit list.
