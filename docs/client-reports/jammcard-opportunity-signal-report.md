# Jammcard Opportunity Signal Report

Prepared: July 5, 2026  
Company: Jammcard  
Focus: Paid live music bookings, artist talent, cultural programming, public events, tourism programs, and creative workforce partnerships.

## Executive Summary

Jammcard has a credible public-sector growth path through three lanes:

1. Public agencies and funded venues buying or funding live music, amphitheater programming, concerts, cultural events, and public-space activation.
2. Arts and culture grantees that need artist talent, production support, teaching artists, or program partners.
3. Event-entertainment and public-concert procurement patterns that point to repeat buyers, incumbent vendors, and future bid cycles.

The most immediate move is to pursue active arts and cultural-program funding before July deadlines, while building a buyer list around parks, tourism, city arts offices, public venues, and funded arts organizations. The strongest near-term CEO action is not broad government sales. It is a focused campaign to 20-30 program owners who already have public money tied to live music, public concerts, cultural programming, or creative workforce programs.

## CEO Handoff: What To Do First

Use this report as a 7-day business development sprint, not as a research memo.

1. Prioritize the NEA July 9 funding window and contact likely arts applicants or recent grantees as a named talent/programming partner.
2. Build a 30-account target list across city arts offices, parks/tourism programs, funded arts organizations, and event-production vendors.
3. Start outreach with a concrete offer: vetted professional musicians, booking support, public event programming, or creative workforce talent infrastructure.
4. Treat USAspending records as buyer and partner intelligence. They show where public money has moved, not automatically open bids.

## Recommended 7-Day Action Plan

### Day 1: Decide the pursuit lanes

Pick three immediate lanes: NEA applicant partnerships, funded city/parks programming, and event-entertainment procurement monitoring. Do not chase every arts record equally.

### Day 2: Build the target list

Create a 30-account list:

- 10 likely NEA applicants or recurring arts grantees
- 8 city/county parks, tourism, and cultural affairs offices
- 6 funded arts organizations with public programming needs
- 6 event-production vendors or agencies tied to public entertainment records

### Day 3: Prepare three outreach notes

Draft one version each for:

- Arts grantees needing stronger project partners
- City/parks/tourism offices programming public spaces
- Event-production vendors that may need musician sourcing support

### Day 4: Pursue urgent grant windows

For the NEA July 9 deadline, contact likely applicants or partners with a simple offer: Jammcard can be added as a talent/programming partner or post-award implementation resource. For the July 6 Creative Tech Exchange deadline, only proceed if a partner is already application-ready.

### Day 5: Start funded-buyer outreach

Prioritize South Carolina Parks, City of Seattle, Jackson Symphony, and ReImagine ATL. The goal is not to sell a generic platform; it is to offer a concrete program support path tied to their funded public programming.

### Day 6: Build the procurement watchlist

Track agencies and vendors connected to event entertainment, public concerts, summer concert series, musician services, and event production. Add renewal dates, likely contracting offices, and incumbent vendors where visible.

### Day 7: CEO review and go/no-go

Review response quality and decide which motion deserves the next two-week sprint:

- Partner with arts/grant recipients
- Sell into city/parks/tourism public programming
- Channel through event-production vendors
- Apply directly for cultural-exchange grants only where eligibility is strong

## Data Limitations

- Source basis: Opportunity Scanner scan data and the public-source records linked in this report.
- The source scan used here was completed June 30, 2026.
- USAspending records are evidence of awarded or funded activity. They are not automatically open solicitations or proof that Jammcard is eligible to receive funds.
- Grants.gov deadlines are time-sensitive. As of July 5, 2026, the Creative Tech Exchange deadline is July 6, 2026 and the NEA Grants for Arts Projects deadline is July 9, 2026.
- Some records identify public funding flow but do not include a source-native decision-maker contact. The recommended contact paths therefore prioritize program offices, procurement offices, recipient leadership, and partner organizations rather than assuming a personal email is available.
