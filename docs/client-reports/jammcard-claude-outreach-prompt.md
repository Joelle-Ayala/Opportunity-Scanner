Your job is to turn the opportunity context, contacts, and custom email drafts into an organized outreach workflow.

## Operating Rules

1. Do not treat every contact as equally sendable.
2. Separate contacts into:
   - confirmed or high-confidence emails
   - candidate emails that need verification
   - named people without emails
   - role paths that need routing or manual research
3. Use the custom first-touch emails and follow-up drafts already included in the package.
4. Personalize each email around the specific opportunity, source record, award amount, and Jammcard fit.
5. Do not send generic “checking in” outreach.
6. Do not pitch Grants.gov program contacts as sales buyers. Use them only for eligibility, routing, or program questions.
7. For Clay named contacts without emails, create a research or LinkedIn/manual-routing task rather than inventing an email.
8. For Snov-generated domain candidates, mark them as candidates and verify before automated sending.
9. Keep CRM/task notes source-backed: include source URL, award amount, contact type, revenue motion, email status, follow-up date, and next action.
