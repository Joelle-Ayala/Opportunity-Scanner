# Opportunity Scanner Social Repurposing System

## Goal

Turn every live article and lead magnet into a source-faithful campaign that can be published across LinkedIn, X/Threads, newsletters, document carousels, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

The source asset remains canonical. Social copy may simplify the idea, but it may not remove a qualification that changes the commercial meaning.

## Pack Standard

Every article pack contains:

- Source, audience, thesis, reusable angles, and proof/caveat notes.
- Three LinkedIn posts.
- One seven-to-ten-post X/Threads thread and one short post.
- One eight-slide carousel with exact copy, caption, visual direction, and alt text.
- Three 30-to-60-second video scripts with spoken copy, on-screen text, shot plan, caption, and CTA.
- Newsletter teaser and destination URL.

Every lead-magnet campaign contains:

- Campaign thesis, audience, promise, eight angles, and a proof/caveat ledger.
- Four LinkedIn posts.
- Two X/Threads threads and two short posts.
- Two eight-slide carousels.
- Four 30-to-75-second video scripts and one 60-to-90-second anchor storyboard.
- Three-email nurture outline and newsletter teaser.

## Claim Rules

1. Never invent a statistic, buyer, award, customer outcome, deadline, quote, or agency endorsement.
2. Keep active opportunities, forecasts, historical awards, funded recipients, policy signals, and general market context labeled separately.
3. A public award is evidence that money moved, not proof that the recipient is currently buying.
4. A grant program is not automatically a direct-apply opportunity for the reader.
5. A company scan can find and organize relevant public records; it cannot promise revenue or complete coverage.
6. Named figures and time-sensitive facts must retain their source and date in the caption or source asset.
7. Do not use agency seals, customer logos, or fabricated product screens in creative.

## Production Formats

- Carousel master: portrait PDF/document layout with stable margins, one idea per slide, and readable mobile type. LinkedIn supports document posts uploaded as PDF and other document formats: https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a518909
- Short-video master: 9:16 vertical, caption-safe framing, spoken copy that works without B-roll, and burned-in captions. Instagram accepts Reels from 1.91:1 through 9:16: https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/1038071743007909
- YouTube version: square or vertical; Shorts up to three minutes are supported, although this program targets 30 to 75 seconds: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/15424877
- Supporting crops: 4:5 feed, 1:1 square, 16:9 thumbnail/preview, and 1200x627 link image.

## Review Gates

1. `drafted`: all required copy and storyboards exist.
2. `claim_reviewed`: every concrete claim can be traced to the source asset or cited evidence.
3. `brand_reviewed`: voice is direct, practical, specific, and free of internal notes.
4. `visual_ready`: slide and shot directions are feasible and accessible.
5. `approved`: founder or delegated editor approved the campaign.
6. `scheduled`: channel, date, owner, link, and UTM are recorded.
7. `published`: live URL and initial performance snapshot are recorded.

No asset should be scheduled before claim review. The production queue is in `production-queue.md`.
