## Verified Proof And Caveat Notes

- **Program/funding status:** [DOT's IIJA Funding Status](https://www.transportation.gov/mission/budget/infrastructure-investment-and-jobs-act-iija-funding-status) distinguishes enacted funding, available funding, announced grants, obligations, and outlays. A selection or obligation does not replace the recipient's procurement.
- **Award data:** [USAspending's Federal Spending Guide](https://www.usaspending.gov/federal-spending-guide) supports award and recipient research. Historical award data does not prove a current budget, open requirement, or repeat purchase.
- **Program/financing evidence:** [Build America Bureau programs](https://www.transportation.gov/buildamerica/financing/programs) can identify eligible public entities, selected recipients, and project-development activity. These are project signals, not open construction contracts.
- **Subcontracting route:** [SBA prime and subcontracting guidance](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-guide/prime-subcontracting) explains subcontracting plans and research routes. A plan or directory does not mean a prime has an available package.
- **Policy/compliance:** [Build America, Buy America](https://www.madeinamerica.gov/resources/financial-assistance/) establishes domestic-preference requirements for covered infrastructure assistance. Applicability and compliance must be checked against the project documents.
- **Notice:** [SAM.gov Contract Opportunities](https://sam.gov/opportunities) provides official notices, dates, attachments, offices, and response instructions. Recheck the latest notice before acting.

## LinkedIn Post 1

An infrastructure funding announcement is not the same thing as a construction opportunity.

A project can move through authorization, grant selection, obligation, design, environmental review, procurement, construction, and closeout. Each stage can point to a different organization and a different action.

For a general contractor, the useful signal may be an owner solicitation. For a specialty trade or materials supplier, it may be a verified prime and supplier route. For an engineering firm, an early planning award may justify monitoring the authority's board, capital program, and consultant procurement pages.

The discipline is simple: record the project stage, public owner, recipient, scope, delivery model, procurement source, and realistic revenue motion separately. Keep capital plans and award records out of the active-bid count until a source controlling procurement appears.

Opportunity intelligence becomes pipeline only when the company can answer: who controls the next purchase, what can we perform, and what official step moves the work forward?

## LinkedIn Post 2

The same bridge, transit, water, or facilities project can create several revenue paths.

The public owner may procure the prime contract. A construction manager may need specialty trades. The selected prime may need materials, equipment, inspection, or technical support. A funded recipient may first procure planning or design. A supplier may need to enter through an approved distributor or prime onboarding process.

That is why a flat "infrastructure leads" list is so hard to use. It hides the role your company can actually play.

A stronger opportunity row identifies the project and stage, the organization controlling the next commercial decision, the package your company could perform, the revenue motion, the official contact route, and one next action. It also records the reason to stop: unsupported geography, licensing, bonding, labor, schedule, domestic-content, or capacity requirements.

The goal is not to attach your company to every infrastructure dollar. It is to find the stage and delivery role where your capabilities have a credible route to revenue.

## LinkedIn Post 3

Construction teams should search beyond SAM.gov, but they should not lower the evidence bar.

Federal notices are only one lane. State DOTs, cities, counties, transit agencies, utilities, airports, ports, school districts, and public authorities often use their own capital pages and procurement portals. USAspending and federal program pages can help identify recipients, historical primes, and projects, but those records do not automatically create live work.

A practical weekly review starts narrow: one geography, one asset class, and one service package. Track project pages and capital plans for demand forming, funding selections for recipient evidence, owner portals for active procurement, and awards for prime or supplier research. Label every lane and recheck status before action.

This protects the sales team from two expensive mistakes: treating a planning announcement as a bid, and pursuing a real project through the wrong organization.

## X / Threads Thread

1. Infrastructure opportunity intelligence is a timing problem. A project can be real and funded without being ready for a contractor response.
2. Separate authorization, grant selection, obligation, design, environmental review, procurement, construction, and closeout. Those stages are not interchangeable.
3. A funding selection can reveal a future public owner or recipient. It does not replace that organization's procurement process.
4. Search the owner layer: state and local capital pages, board records, project websites, vendor portals, and current solicitations.
5. Then choose the route. Prime contractor, specialty trade, supplier, equipment vendor, professional service, and recipient partner are different motions.
6. Match the contact path to the route: contracting office, public works, capital delivery, procurement, or a verified prime supplier channel.
7. Check capacity before pursuit: geography, license, bonding, insurance, labor, schedule, equipment, past performance, and any domestic-preference rules.
8. Keep plans, selections, and historical awards out of active bid counts until current procurement evidence exists.
9. The best next action may be bid review, supplier onboarding, recipient research, a monitoring date, or "do not pursue." That honesty is the point.
