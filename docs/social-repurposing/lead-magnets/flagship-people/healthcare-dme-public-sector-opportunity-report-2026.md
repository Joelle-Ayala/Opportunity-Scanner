## Campaign Thesis

Healthcare public-sector growth is a set of different lanes, not one market. VA procurement, Medicare supplier readiness, funded providers, grants, distributors, primes, and policy signals each require a distinct target, constraint check, Revenue Motion, and next action. The campaign wins by helping teams choose the right lane before they chase the record.

## Audience

- DMEPOS suppliers and medical-supply manufacturers or distributors
- Rehabilitation, mobility, orthotics, and prosthetics businesses
- Healthcare technology and implementation companies
- Revenue, channel, compliance, and business development leaders evaluating public-sector routes

## Lead-Capture Promise

Get a six-lane guide to VA procurement, Medicare supplier signals, funded providers, recipients, distributors, and source-backed next actions. The report does not determine enrollment, reimbursement, accreditation, coding, clinical compliance, contracting eligibility, or revenue potential.

## Eight Campaign Angles

1. **Healthcare is not one opportunity lane:** Separate procurement, reimbursement, funding, channels, grants, and policy.
2. **Compliance is not a buyer lead:** A Medicare requirement can be urgent without supporting sales outreach.
3. **The 2026 moratorium check:** Time-sensitive CMS status must be verified before enrollment action.
4. **Schedule coverage is not an order:** VA categories show a route, not current demand.
5. **Funded provider, not automatic buyer:** Validate scope, period, purchasing authority, and product fit.
6. **Round 2028 is a readiness signal:** Target dates and covered categories do not mean bidding is open.
7. **Buyer versus readiness decision tree:** Route records to Pursue, Validate, Monitor, or Reject.
8. **Channel-first healthcare growth:** A schedule holder, distributor, manufacturer, or prime may be the practical route.

### LinkedIn 2: Compliance is not demand

A compliance requirement can be a high-priority business task without being a sales lead.

CMS supplier enrollment, accreditation, fee schedules, claims data, and competitive-bidding guidance can reveal market structure and readiness requirements. They do not automatically identify a buyer or open opportunity.

That distinction matters in 2026. The source report notes CMS's February 27 temporary nationwide moratorium on specified initial DMEPOS medical-supply company enrollments. CMS said it would last six months and could be extended. Its current status and affected definitions must be checked before any enrollment action.

The report also treats Round 2028 as a readiness lane, not an open contract. Product-category, location, accreditation, licensing, and enrollment records may need work now, while registration and bidding dates still require official confirmation.

### LinkedIn 3: Schedule coverage is not an order

Finding your product category on a government schedule is useful. It is not proof that an agency is buying.

VA Schedule 65 II A covers medical equipment and supplies across several categories. That can help a healthcare company map products, codes, solicitation requirements, and possible ordering routes.

The commercial question comes next: Is there an active notice? Does the company hold the required route? Is a distributor or schedule holder better positioned? Does a historical award reveal an incumbent? Are product, geography, delivery, and compliance requirements manageable?

A schedule page can support direct-route research, a Channel / Distributor Motion, or partner qualification. It cannot support a claim that an order exists.

The free 2026 Healthcare and DME Public-Sector Opportunity Report shows how to compare VA procurement, Medicare readiness, funded providers, recipients, primes, distributors, and grants without collapsing them into one pipeline.

Download the report and verify current VA schedule materials, SAM.gov notices, codes, and ordering instructions before action.

### LinkedIn 4: A funded provider is a hypothesis

A funded health center or healthcare grant recipient may be a buyer, implementation site, partner, or simply a useful research target.

The award alone does not tell you which role applies.

Before outreach, verify the program purpose, project period, geography, recipient role, approved scope, and likely purchasing authority. Then separate the clinical user, economic buyer, procurement route, and compliance owner. Those roles may sit in different teams or organizations.

Your opening question should test scope and process without implying that grant money is unrestricted or available for your product.

HRSA data is valuable because it can name programs, recipients, awards, and health-center sites. The source report also warns that FY2026 data is preliminary and that an active grant can be defined by its project end date, not proof of new current-year funding.
