# Opportunity Scanner Blog Data, Visual, and Repurposing System

## Purpose

Every Opportunity Scanner post should become a research-backed content asset that can support SEO, AEO, social publishing, newsletter growth, outbound, and paid-report conversion.

The goal is not to publish more content. The goal is to make each post useful, credible, visual, and reusable across channels.

## Per-Post Asset Package

For every post, create an internal package with:

- Source article URL and slug.
- Primary ICP pain point.
- Primary keyword and secondary keywords.
- Core thesis.
- Source list with URLs, retrieval dates, and notes.
- Data points used in the article.
- Chart or graph recommendations.
- Featured image prompt.
- Supporting visual prompts.
- Medium adaptation.
- Substack/newsletter adaptation.
- LinkedIn Article adaptation.
- X long-form/article adaptation.
- LinkedIn text post.
- LinkedIn carousel outline.
- X thread.
- Stat/source post.
- Quote post only when a real named-person quote exists.
- Outbound hook for one ICP segment.
- UTM link map.
- Review status: draft, source-reviewed, visual-ready, approved, published, repurposed.

Keep this package internal. Do not show production labels on the public article page.

## Data Storytelling Standard

Each post should include data research before final publish.

Preferred data sources:

- SBA for small-business contracting and procurement access.
- USAspending.gov for awards, recipients, agencies, and money already moved.
- SAM.gov for active procurement and opportunity notices.
- Grants.gov for grant programs and available funding.
- Federal Register and Regulations.gov for policy and regulatory demand.
- Treasury for state/local funding flows.
- BLS for workforce and labor-market context.
- Census for market, business, geography, and demographic context.
- BEA for industry contribution, GDP, and economic activity.
- CMS, DOL, Department of Education, Energy, Transportation, VA, NEA, NSF, and other agencies for vertical-specific context.

Use charts when they clarify the story:

- Bar chart: compare funding amounts, sector sizes, agency spend, or program totals.
- Line chart: show trend over time.
- Stacked bar: show category mix.
- Map: show geographic spread when data is location-based.
- Table: compare source types, revenue motions, or contact paths.
- Flow diagram: show how a public record becomes an action row.
- Opportunity matrix: compare actionability, timing, contact path, and revenue motion.

Do not fabricate data. If a visual is conceptual rather than measured, label it internally as illustrative and do not present it as a real statistic.

## Visual and Media Standard

Stock images should be temporary placeholders only.

Preferred visual workflow:

1. Define the article's buyer/operator scene.
2. Define the data story or framework visual.
3. Generate a unique editorial featured image with AI image generation.
4. Create supporting visuals for charts, frameworks, or opportunity table examples.
5. Use Canva for final layout, carousel packaging, resizing, and brand consistency when needed.
6. Use Higgsfield-style video/motion tools for short visual explainers when the post has strong social or launch value.

Visual requirements:

- No fake agency seals.
- No fake customer logos.
- No misleading fake dashboards.
- No implied endorsement from cited agencies.
- No invented charts.
- Charts must cite source and date.
- Product UI previews should be clearly representative unless they show actual product output.

## Republishing Requirements

Each post should have a channel-specific adaptation, not a copy-paste duplicate.

### Medium

- Use a stronger editorial headline.
- Keep the article self-contained.
- Add canonical link back to the Opportunity Scanner post when possible.
- Include one short product CTA at the end.

### Substack

- Make it more founder-led and conversational.
- Include one practical takeaway list.
- Include one visual or chart.
- End with a scan/report CTA.

### LinkedIn Article

- Lead with the business problem and revenue outcome.
- Use skimmable sections and a stronger founder POV.
- Include one chart, framework, or opportunity table visual.
- Tag relevant people or organizations only when appropriate and truthful.

### X Long-Form / Article

- Use a sharper hook and shorter paragraphs.
- Include one chart or visual.
- Use links to the source article and scan page.
- Avoid overloading with citations; keep the citations in the source article.

## Social Repurposing Requirements

Every post should produce:

- LinkedIn carousel: 6-8 slides.
- LinkedIn text post: 900-1,300 characters.
- X thread: 6-10 posts.
- X short post: one stat or contrarian claim.
- Newsletter snippet: 150-250 words.
- Outbound hook: 80-140 words for a relevant ICP segment.
- Visual crop list: 1:1, 4:5, 16:9, and 1200x627 where useful.
