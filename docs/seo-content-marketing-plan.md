# Opportunity Scanner SEO and Content Marketing Plan

Last updated: July 9, 2026

## Chief of Staff Recommendation

The website should move from "AI scans your website for opportunities" to:

> Public-sector money is an overlooked revenue channel. Opportunity Scanner turns your company website into sourced opportunity signals, buyer and partner targets, contact paths, and next best actions.

This is the strongest wedge because most SMB, mid-market, and even larger companies do not think of government spending, grants, procurement, policy, workforce funding, reimbursement, or funded buyers as a normal sales channel. Existing tools mostly speak to experienced government contractors, grant teams, or public affairs teams. Opportunity Scanner should own the beginner-to-business-development bridge.

## Core ICP Insight

The ICP is not only "companies already looking for grants" or "government contractors."

The larger ICP is companies that:

- already sell something commercially
- have a website that explains their product, service, vertical, or target customer
- do not know whether public-sector demand exists for what they sell
- do not have a dedicated government business development team
- would act if public-sector opportunities were translated into buyer targets, revenue motions, contact paths, and outreach-ready next steps

The buyer pain is not a lack of raw data. The data exists across SAM.gov, Grants.gov, USAspending.gov, Federal Register, agency pages, state/local portals, and enrichment providers. The pain is translation: "What does this mean for my company, who should I pursue, and what should I do next?"

### ICP Bridge Copy

Most companies treat government spending as too confusing, too slow, or irrelevant to their business. Opportunity Scanner shows where public money is already moving, who is funded, what is opening now, and what your sales or partnerships team should do next.

### Product Promise

Scan your website. Find relevant public-sector opportunity signals. Get sourced targets, contact paths, outreach angles, and next best actions.

## What To Avoid

Do not position Opportunity Scanner as:

- an AI grant finder
- a generic government database search
- an instant government contract generator
- a static research memo
- a raw SAM.gov or Grants.gov wrapper
- a tool that always tries to find a personal email
- a product only for existing government contractors

Use these terms instead:

- public-sector revenue signals
- opportunity intelligence
- new deal-flow channel
- buyer, funder, partner, agency, grantee, and award-recipient targets
- contact path
- next best action
- CRM-ready opportunity rows
- outreach package

## Proof Points and Market Context

- SAM.gov describes contract opportunities as procurement notices from federal contracting offices that anyone interested in doing business with the government can search. Source: https://sam.gov/opportunities
- Grants.gov exposes funding opportunities with search filters like opportunity status, funding instrument type, eligibility, category, and agency. Source: https://www.grants.gov/search-grants
- SBA's small business procurement scorecard explains that agencies are measured against small-business contracting goals, and the aggregate target is 23% of federal contracting dollars. Source: https://www.sba.gov/document/support-small-business-procurement-scorecard-overview
- Deltek GovWin positions around better pursuit decisions, early signals, agency history, and competitive context for established government contractors. Source: https://www.deltek.com/products/govwin/
- HigherGov positions around government market intelligence, contracts, grants, opportunities, agencies, awardees, people, and capture tools. Source: https://www.highergov.com/

Opportunity Scanner's white space is not "more data than GovWin/HigherGov." The white space is making public-sector demand legible and actionable for companies that are not yet sophisticated government capture teams.

## First Article Briefs

### 1. Why Government Spending Is the Most Overlooked Growth Channel for Businesses

Audience: founders, revenue leaders, consultants, agencies, SMB operators.

Thesis: Public-sector money is not just contracts and grants. It is demand evidence: agencies buying, grantees receiving money, primes winning awards, workforce programs launching, policy priorities shifting, and reimbursement pathways changing.

Outline:

1. Most businesses ignore public-sector demand because it feels slow and confusing.
2. Public money moves through several channels: direct procurement, grants, funded buyers, award recipients, primes, workforce programs, policy, and reimbursement.
3. The problem is not finding data; it is translating data into next actions.
4. What a public-sector opportunity signal looks like.
5. How Opportunity Scanner turns a company website into a prioritized opportunity table.

CTA: Scan your company website to find public-sector revenue signals.

### 2. Can My Business Sell to the Government? A Practical First Check

Audience: companies curious but inexperienced with public-sector sales.

Thesis: A company does not need to become a full government contractor on day one. The first question is whether there is public-sector demand adjacent to the product or service.

Outline:

1. Direct agency sales are only one route.
2. Other routes: sell to funded buyers, partner with recipients, sell through distributors, contact primes, monitor policy, apply directly when eligible.
3. Signs your company may have public-sector fit.
4. Signs public-sector is not a good immediate fit.
5. Use a scan as a lightweight market test.

CTA: Run a free scan.

### 3. What Is a Public-Sector Opportunity Signal?

Audience: educational/search.

Thesis: An opportunity signal is sourced evidence that public money, demand, or policy attention is moving in a direction that could create revenue, partnership, funding, or procurement opportunity.

Outline:

1. Definition.
2. Signal types: money already moved, money available now, policy demand coming, workforce/market context, reimbursement/contextual signals.
3. Why raw signals are not enough.
4. How actionability, revenue motion, contact path, and next best action make signals useful.
5. Examples by vertical.

CTA: See what signals match your company.

### 4. Grants vs Contracts vs Funded Buyers

Audience: companies confused by public-sector categories.

Thesis: The best opportunity is not always the grant or bid itself. Sometimes it is the organization that received money, the agency that funds a program, the prime contractor, or the policy office that signals future demand.

Outline:

1. Grants: direct apply versus sell to grantees.
2. Contracts: active solicitations versus award history.
3. Funded buyers: organizations with money and mission-aligned demand.
4. Policy/workforce/reimbursement as early demand signals.
5. How to decide what to do next.

CTA: Turn public-sector records into a prioritized action table.

### 5. How to Find Funded Buyers Before You Start Cold Outreach

Audience: sales teams and founders.

Thesis: Cold outreach gets better when it starts from source-backed evidence that a target has received money, bought similar services, or operates inside a funded program.

Outline:

1. Why generic cold lists are weak.
2. How award recipients, grantees, primes, and agencies become target accounts.
3. What evidence to include in outreach.
4. Contact path does not always mean personal email.
5. How to turn a signal into CRM-ready next steps.

CTA: Generate an outreach-ready opportunity package.
