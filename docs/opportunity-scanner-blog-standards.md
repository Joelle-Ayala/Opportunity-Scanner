# Opportunity Scanner Blog Standards

Adapted from the SchoolGig blog repurposing standard for Opportunity Scanner.

## Goal

Every post should help the ICP understand public-sector revenue as a practical growth channel and should also create reusable assets for social, outbound, and paid-report conversion.

Each post should be treated as a research-backed content asset, not only a website page. The source article should be strong enough to publish on the Opportunity Scanner site and adaptable into Medium, Substack, LinkedIn Articles, X long-form/articles, short social posts, carousels, outbound hooks, and newsletter content.

## Required Public Article Elements

Each public resource post should include:

- A clear ICP pain point connected to Opportunity Scanner's USP.
- A practical list, checklist, framework, or step-by-step workflow.
- Relevant stats or proof points from reputable sources, integrated into the article narrative where they support a claim.
- A real named-person quote only when a credible public person is quoted directly and the quote advances the point of the paragraph.
- Source citations when the supporting source is an organization, database, documentation page, or public portal rather than a person.
- At least one data storytelling element when the topic supports it: chart, graph, benchmark table, signal map, source comparison, market-size snapshot, or workflow diagram.
- A unique AI-generated featured image direction and, where useful, supporting visuals throughout the article.

Do not show internal production labels in the public article. Terms like "X thread hook," "quote post," "carousel-ready," "repurposing block," "suggested social tags," and "featured image prompt" belong in internal content operations files, not on the website.

## Content Pattern

Use this structure for most posts:

1. Problem the ICP recognizes.
2. Why public-sector data is confusing or underused.
3. Practical framework or checklist.
4. Stats, source evidence, or a credible named-person quote woven into the argument when it truly supports the point.
5. How Opportunity Scanner turns the issue into action rows.
6. CTA to scan a company website or view a sample report.

Internal repurposing assets should be generated after the article is drafted and stored in docs, campaign files, or a content calendar. They should not appear in the public blog template.

## Quality Rules

- Do not publish thin SEO-only posts.
- Do not invent stats, quotes, contacts, customer results, or endorsements.
- Do not fabricate data for charts or graphs. Use sourced public data, product-generated aggregate data, or clearly labeled illustrative frameworks that do not claim to be real measurements.
- Use public-source quotes only with citations and only label them as quotes when a person is actually being quoted.
- If the support comes from SAM.gov, Grants.gov, USAspending.gov, or another official source page, label it as a source note, evidence, or proof point instead of a quote.
- Do not publish placeholder phrases such as "source-backed quote," "quote post," or "stat post" inside the article body.
- Do not add a quote just to satisfy a format. If a named, relevant, reputable quote is not available, use official source evidence and explain why it matters.
- Do not imply a cited agency or publication endorses Opportunity Scanner.
- Prefer official sources such as SAM.gov, Grants.gov, USAspending.gov, SBA, BLS, Census, CMS, DOL, Federal Register, and agency pages.
- When a claim is useful but not yet sourced, mark it internally as citation-needed before publishing.
- When a chart is created from public data, store the source URL, retrieval date, metric definition, and transformation notes in the internal repurposing package.
- When a visual is illustrative rather than data-derived, label it internally as illustrative and avoid presenting it as measured fact.

## Social Repurposing Requirements

Each post should be able to become the following in the internal content workflow:

- Medium article.
- Substack/newsletter post.
- LinkedIn Article.
- X long-form/article.
- LinkedIn carousel.
- X thread.
- LinkedIn text post.
- Stat post.
- Quote post.
- Newsletter snippet.
- Outbound hook for a relevant industry or ICP segment.
- One or more chart/social visual crops when supporting data exists.

These assets should be stored separately from the public article or exposed only in internal/admin contexts.

## Featured Image Direction

Use AI-generated visuals that show business operators, founders, sales teams, service providers, healthcare operators, education/workforce teams, event producers, manufacturers, or nonprofit leaders using intelligence to make revenue decisions.

Preferred visual types:

- Editorial featured image with real buyer/operator context.
- Product-style opportunity table preview with no fake customer logos or fake source records.
- Simple sourced chart or graph that supports the argument.
- Signal map showing how public records become buyer, partner, funding, or workflow actions.
- Carousel-ready visual explaining one framework from the article.

Use image generation, Canva, Higgsfield-style motion/media tools, or other approved creative tools where appropriate. The output should feel like a modern B2B SaaS publication, not generic stock imagery.

Avoid:

- Generic government buildings as the default visual.
- Abstract gradients with no human or product context.
- Fake agency seals, fake logos, fake dashboards with misleading numbers, or customer-implied visuals.
- Charts with unsourced, invented, or misleading data.

## Acceptance Criteria

A post is publish-ready when it:

- Normally provides at least 1,200 words of useful depth for a strategic guide; shorter formats require an explicit editorial reason and must never inflate their read time.
- Teaches something useful even if the reader does not buy.
- Makes public-sector opportunity feel concrete.
- Includes at least one reusable list/framework.
- Includes proof or quotes where available.
- Includes a data/storytelling visual when credible data exists.
- Connects naturally to Opportunity Scanner's action-table value.
- Has a unique AI-generated featured image or approved custom visual direction.
- Has an internal repurposing package available outside the public post.
