# Product Marketing Launch Messaging

Date: 2026-07-09

Owner: Product Strategy & Product Marketing Agent

## Launch Positioning

Opportunity Scanner turns a company website into source-backed public-sector opportunity intelligence.

The product should not be framed as a grant finder, a government database search tool, or an AI summary of a company's website. The stronger launch frame is:

> Public-sector money is a deal-flow channel most companies are not tracking. Opportunity Scanner finds relevant signals, explains the revenue motion, identifies buyer or partner targets, and tells the team what to do next.

The core promise:

> Scan your website. Find where public money, procurement, policy, grants, workforce programs, reimbursement, and funded buyers create revenue paths for your company. Get sourced opportunity rows with the target, motion, contact path, and next best action.

## Sharper ICP Framing

### Primary ICP

B2B founders, operators, consultants, and revenue teams at companies that already sell a product or service commercially, but do not have a dedicated public-sector business development function.

They are not waking up asking for a "grant finder." They are asking:

- Could government, education, healthcare, workforce, arts, infrastructure, or public funding create demand for what we sell?
- Are agencies, grantees, primes, funded buyers, or program offices already spending in our category?
- Is there a realistic next step, or is this just research noise?
- Who should we pursue, partner with, monitor, or add to CRM?

### Best-Fit Segments

- Public-sector adjacent B2B companies in healthcare, rehab, DME, education, workforce, manufacturing, arts/culture, SaaS, compliance, and professional services.
- Agencies and consultants that want to produce credible opportunity reports for clients.
- Sales and growth teams that need source-backed target accounts, trigger events, and CRM-ready notes.
- Operators exploring whether government spending, grants, funded buyers, or policy shifts can become a new channel.

### ICP Exclusions

Opportunity Scanner should not over-index on:

- Experienced government contractors that already use full capture platforms and expect deep bid management.
- Consumers or nonprofits only looking for personal assistance grants.
- Users who only want a raw searchable database.
- Teams that expect guaranteed contracts, grants, or instant personal email discovery.

## Revenue Outcome Language

Use language that makes the product feel like a revenue workflow, not a research report.

Preferred phrases:

- "Find public-sector deal flow your team is not tracking."
- "Turn public money movement into buyer and partner targets."
- "See whether government spending, grants, policy, or funded buyers create a revenue path for your company."
- "Move from source record to next best action."
- "Build a public-sector pursuit plan without hiring a GovCon team first."
- "Give sales, partnerships, or founder-led growth a sourced reason to act."
- "Create CRM-ready opportunity rows from public-sector signals."
- "Know whether to apply, sell to an agency, sell to a funded buyer, partner with a recipient, monitor policy, or research the market."

Avoid:

- "AI grant finder"
- "Government search results"
- "We scrape public data"
- "Instant contract opportunities"
- "Find emails for government buyers"
- "Generic recommendations"
- "Raw database results"

The paid unlock should sell actionability, not volume. "More signals" is weaker than "a pursuit plan."

### Primary Unlock Message

> Unlock the full opportunity pipeline: all prioritized public-sector signals, source-backed evidence, buyer and partner targets, contact paths, CRM notes, outreach angles, and workflow-ready payloads.

### Paid Value Props

- All prioritized opportunity signals, not just the free preview.
- Source links and source-backed evidence for credibility.
- Buyer, partner, agency, grantee, award-recipient, or program-office targets.
- Revenue motion for each signal so the user knows how value could be captured.

## Objection Handling

### "We do not sell to government."

You may not need to sell directly to an agency. Public-sector demand can show up through funded buyers, grantees, award recipients, prime vendors, distributors, workforce programs, policy priorities, or reimbursement pathways.

### "We are not a government contractor."

Opportunity Scanner is built for companies before they have a formal public-sector team. It helps answer whether the channel is worth exploring and which path is realistic.

### "Grants are not our business model."

The product is not limited to grants. A grant can be a direct application, but it can also identify funded organizations that may buy, partner, train, procure, distribute, or expand services.

### "Public-sector sales are too slow."

Some paths are slow, but not all signals require a bid. The report separates active opportunities, funded buyers, award recipients, partner motions, policy monitoring, and research-only paths so teams can choose the right level of effort.

### "We do not know who to contact."

The right contact path is not always a personal email. It may be a procurement office, program office, source-native contact, grants manager, grantee, prime vendor, distributor, partner, or manual research task.

### "Public data is already searchable."

The value is not raw access. The value is translation: which records matter for this company, what revenue motion they imply, who the target is, and what the next best action should be.

### "Will this guarantee revenue?"

No. Opportunity Scanner should be positioned as opportunity intelligence and pursuit planning. It reduces discovery time, identifies credible paths, and helps teams decide what to pursue.

## Launch Recommendations

1. Make the homepage promise about public-sector deal flow, not AI research.
2. Put revenue motion and next best action language above generic "insights" language.
3. Treat the Opportunity Action Table as the main product proof.
4. Sell the paid unlock as a full opportunity pipeline and pursuit plan.
5. Keep contact language broad enough to include offices, source-native contacts, recipients, partners, and manual research.
6. Use content to educate companies that do not yet know public-sector demand can be a channel.
7. Keep source credibility visible without exposing raw connector or debug language.
8. Prioritize messaging that helps a founder decide: pursue, partner, monitor, research, or ignore.
