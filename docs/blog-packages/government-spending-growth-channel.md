## Core thesis

Government spending is useful to a business before the business is ready to bid on a contract or apply for a grant. Official records can reveal four different kinds of commercial evidence:

1. Agencies and offices buying products or services.
2. Organizations and prime contractors that already received public money.
3. Open or forecasted funding and procurement activity.
4. Programs and procurement forecasts that indicate demand a team should monitor.

The opportunity is not the raw record. The opportunity is the business-development decision produced from it: target, revenue motion, contact path, and next best action.

## ICP pain and desired outcome

### Primary pain

Founders and revenue teams often treat government spending as a specialized government-contracting category because public records are fragmented across procurement notices, award data, grants, agency forecasts, and program pages. Even when the data is public, it does not arrive as a prioritized sales task.

### Secondary pains

- The team cannot tell whether a record is active, historical, forecasted, or merely contextual.
- Grants and contracts are treated as the only paths, so funded buyers, primes, recipients, and partner routes are missed.
- Source records do not explain whom to pursue or what to do next.
- A generic contact list can produce the wrong outreach route.
- The team needs a lightweight market test before investing in registrations, certifications, capture resources, or a dedicated public-sector sales function.

### Desired reader outcome

The reader should leave with a five-part method for turning a public record into an opportunity row:

1. Verify the source and timing.
2. Identify the likely target organization.
3. Choose the revenue motion.
4. Choose the contact path.
5. Assign a next best action.

## Audience priority

1. B2B founders and operators testing non-obvious revenue channels.

1. **Establish that a real market exists.** Open with the SBA's FY2024 small-business contracting figures. State precisely that this is one federal contracting slice, not the total universe of public spending.
2. **Break the bid-only mental model.** Use SBA's prime/subcontracting distinction to show that direct contracting is only one participation route.

4. **Translate evidence into commercial choices.** Show that the same record can imply different motions: sell to an agency, sell to a funded buyer, sell to an award recipient, partner with a recipient, apply, register, monitor, or research.
